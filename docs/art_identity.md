# Chakana Art Identity

## Purpose
This document defines the canonical visual grammar of the Chakana universe before any large-scale art generation. The goal is to keep the game readable, mythic, and internally coherent across cards, portraits, props, scenes, and effects.

## Identity Pillars
- Legibility first: the player must identify subject, object, rarity frame, and action intent at a glance.
- Andean sacred fantasy: stepped geometry, ritual stone, solar glyphs, void sigils, mountain massing, and ceremonial silhouettes.
- Mythic hierarchy: subject first, sacred object second, environment third, effects last.
- Stylized structure over blobs: all entities must be built from controlled shape grammar, not random rectangles or undirected masses.

## Core Shape Language
### Global rules
- Use large readable primary masses first, then medium symbolic masses, then small accent detail.
- Avoid final silhouettes made from plain rectangles, circles, or unbroken columns.
- Prefer stepped silhouettes, tapering masses, split torso planes, and clear positive/negative space.
- Curves signal wisdom, sacred flow, fauna, and calm power.
- Angles signal solar force, ritual authority, danger, and martial intent.
- Void or corruption motifs may distort shape language, but must stay readable from the card viewport.

### Silhouette rules
- One dominant silhouette per card.
- Subject silhouette must remain readable at thumbnail size.
- Head, torso, pelvis, and prop must read as separate masses.
- Weapons and sacred props must never erase the torso silhouette.
- Halo and symbol layers must sit behind or above the subject core, not replace it.

## Composition Rules
### Canvas and safe zone
- Output resolution: 1920x1080.
- Composition base: 480x270.
- Safe art zone: 70 percent of the canvas.
- Subjects must stay inside the safe art zone.

### Subject-object-background hierarchy
- Subject occupancy target: 0.22 to 0.40.
- Object occupancy target: 0.06 to 0.25.
- Background visual influence target: about 35 percent.
- Subject anchor modes allowed: center, lower_center, golden_ratio.
- Subject must read first, object second, symbol third, environment fourth, FX last.

### Layering order
1. Background
2. Environment
3. Symbol behind subject
4. FX back
5. Subject core
6. Subject detail/costume
7. Object/weapon
8. FX front
9. Grade and frame overlay

## Scale Rules
### Character ratios
- Head ratio: 0.16 to 0.18 of subject height.
- Torso ratio: 0.30 to 0.34.
- Pelvis ratio: 0.10 to 0.14.
- Legs ratio: 0.34 to 0.40.
- Arms ratio: 0.30 to 0.36.
- Shoulder span: 1.7 to 2.0 head widths.

### Prop ratios
- Weapon length: 0.45 to 0.65 of subject height.
- Weapon thickness: 0.06 to 0.12 of weapon length.
- Symbol size: 0.18 to 0.30 of subject height.
- Halo radius: 0.45 to 0.75 of subject height.

## Color Palette System
### Global palette structure
Each image should use:
- 1 dominant hue family
- 1 secondary hue family
- 1 highlight/accent hue
- 1 shadow family
- 1 ritual or energy accent only if needed

### Material palette rules
- Cloth: broad midtone, deeper shadow, limited edge light.
- Metal: darker body, bright directional highlight, narrow trim accents.
- Stone: matte planes, dusty highlights, low saturation neutrals.
- Wood: warm dark base, subtle grain accent, limited gloss.
- Glow: reserved for symbols, void energy, solar charge, or sacred aura. Never flatten the whole scene.

## Pixel Density and Surface Readability
- Final art should read as high-density stylized card art, not raw pixel art.
- Detail density must be concentrated on face, torso center, prop glyph, and sacred symbol.
- Avoid noise blankets, airbrush haze, or equal-detail-everywhere rendering.
- Surface finish should describe material planes, not blur silhouette edges.

## Camera Framing
- Default framing: medium close to medium full, centered on the subject.
- Heroic attack shots may push low-angle perspective, but must keep the torso readable.
- Ritual or contemplative shots may use vertical framing emphasis and golden-ratio anchor placement.
- Environment should support the camera, not replace the subject.

## Environment Layering
- Foreground is optional and should only frame the subject.
- Midground carries the subject platform, ritual site, temple step, ridge, or sacred floor.
- Background establishes biome, sky, void, sea, ruins, or mountain scale.
- Lighting direction must reinforce subject volume, not bleach it.

## Subject-Object Hierarchy
- Subject is the narrative center.
- Object is the action or role key.
- Symbol explains cosmology or faction.
- Effects reinforce mood and power state.
- Environment establishes place and civilization context.

## Faction and Archetype Language
### Solar Warriors
- Shape language: triangular torso, broad shoulders, stable lower body, strong diagonal action lines.
- Silhouette: heroic and open, never collapsed inward.
- Props: spear, sword, radiant standard, shield-like sacred geometry.
- Palette: gold, amber, ivory, warm red accents.
- Lighting: directional, sunrise or high solar edge light.
- Biomes: mountain citadels, high plateaus, temple ridges, sunlit ruins.

### Archons
- Shape language: vertical, severe, cathedral-like, narrow head, sharper shoulders.
- Silhouette: ritual column broken by cloak planes, sigil structures, or stepped void masses.
- Props: staff, decree rod, chained sigil, void relic.
- Palette: obsidian, crimson, toxic green, ash white sparingly.
- Lighting: backlit void, eclipse glow, severe rim light, low ambient warmth.
- Biomes: void sanctums, broken observatories, cosmic temples, corrupted ritual chambers.

### Guide Mages
- Shape language: soft vertical flow, calmer shoulders, cloth layering, symbolic support props.
- Silhouette: upright, poised, open chest, gentle robe split, visible support hand or focus tool.
- Props: orb, codex, staff, seal tablet, divination instrument.
- Palette: teal, pearl, indigo, gold accents.
- Lighting: sacred soft light, temple bounce, mystic cyan rim.
- Biomes: sanctuaries, mountain temples, ritual plateaus, sacred skies.

### Creatures
- Shape language: one strong body core plus one memorable mutation or sacred motif.
- Silhouette: readable animal basis first, mythic variation second.
- Palette: tied to biome or faction, never random rainbow variation.
- Motifs: condor, puma, deer, serpent, void beast, sacred guardian.

### Sacred Objects
- Shape language: iconic glyphs, stepped ornaments, ceremonial symmetry with one asymmetrical accent when needed.
- Readability: object silhouette must read without relying on FX.
- Materials: metal, carved stone, lacquered wood, radiant crystal, void mineral.

### Biomes
- Mountains: stepped horizons, cold air depth, sacred stone platforms.
- Forest/jungle: dense layered foliage, humid atmosphere, ritual clearings.
- Sea: broad horizon, reflective planes, wind direction, cold or mystic tide.
- Ruins/temples: vertical markers, stairs, columns, altars, observatories.
- Void: sparse but high-contrast, symbolic geometry, aurora-like fracture light.

## Non-negotiable Production Rules
- Art generation must be frameless.
- Frame overlay belongs only to card render stage.
- No subject can be approved if the silhouette collapses into an undifferentiated blob.
- No effect layer can dominate the subject core.
- No object can float without a visible anchor relationship to the subject.

## Approval Criteria Before Mass Production
- Subject readable at card scale.
- Subject, prop, and environment each occupy distinct roles.
- Archetype identity is obvious in silhouette before detail.
- Palette supports civilization and role identity.
- Scene remains legible after frame overlay.
