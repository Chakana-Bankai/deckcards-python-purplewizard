# Chakana Audio Identity

## Purpose
This document defines the canonical sonic identity of Chakana before any large-scale soundtrack regeneration. The goal is to move the project away from monotonous loops and toward a recognizable sacred-fantasy musical language with melodic identity, ritual atmosphere, and gameplay readability.

## Identity Pillars
- Sacred Andean fantasy first: the soundtrack must feel ceremonial, elevated, ancient, and cosmological.
- Melody over drone-only loops: ambience may sustain, but musical identity requires note movement, motif recall, and interval logic.
- Role clarity by context: menu, map, combat, boss, shop, rewards, and reveals must sound meaningfully different.
- Mix readability: no layer stack should become muddy, fatiguing, or flat over long loops.

## Core Musical Scale
### Primary scale family
- Core identity scale: minor pentatonic with added second and sixth for sacred lift.
- Practical pitch language: use a six-note mode that supports mystic gravity without collapsing into pure sadness.
- Main Chakana tension colors: minor second for ritual friction, perfect fourth for sacred ascent, minor sixth for mythic longing.

### Harmonic rules
- Favor open fifths, suspended chords, quartal movement, and drone foundations.
- Avoid dense jazz harmony, glossy pop cadences, and overly heroic major-triad repetition.
- Resolution should feel ceremonial or cosmic, not cheerful by default.

## Instrument Palette
### Core instruments
- Airy choir pads
- Breath flute / quena-like lead colors
- Low ritual drones
- Hand percussion / tribal drum beds
- Frame drum and tom accents
- Bell, chime, or struck stone transients
- Warm plucked strings or charango-like accents in lighter contexts
- Subtle synth brass or hybrid horn layers only for combat escalation

### Forbidden defaults
- No generic EDM supersaw leads
- No piano demo-loop identity as main voice
- No full orchestral Hollywood wall for every context
- No single sine/square drone as final musical solution

## Sacred Tone Identity
- The world should sound ancient, wind-touched, ceremonial, and cosmic.
- Solar energy sounds radiant and percussive.
- Archon energy sounds cold, ritualized, severe, and fractured.
- Guide/Oracle energy sounds breath-based, circular, reflective, and luminous.
- Sacred symbols should read as bells, glass harmonics, stone resonance, filtered choir light, or controlled shimmer.

## Ambient Texture Style
- Ambience must support place and myth, not behave like static broadband noise.
- Wind should carry direction.
- Temple ambiences should imply architecture and reverberant stone.
- Void ambiences should feel sparse, distant, and unstable, with symbolic pulses instead of constant wash.
- Layering should be slow and spacious, with a clear low, mid, and high role.

## Combat Rhythm Language
- Combat must pulse with intention, not just tempo.
- Use repeating rhythmic cells with variation every 2 to 4 bars.
- Percussion should imply ritual force and tactical pressure, not random drum spam.
- Strong beats should align with card-play drama and enemy intent.
- Boss contexts should widen the low end, increase ceremonial threat, and stretch phrase tension.

## Global Structural Rules
- Every loop must have multiple distinct notes.
- Every loop must have at least one phrase variation before repeating.
- Every context must have one identity layer, one support layer, and one texture layer.
- Loops should feel seamless without sounding frozen.
- Stingers may be short, but must still contain contour and shape, not only impact noise.

## Context Definitions
### studio_intro
- Role: ritual signature on boot.
- Scale mood: mysterious but welcoming.
- Texture: breath, chime, distant stone resonance.
- Motion: short motif statement, then ceremonial resolution.

### main_menu
- Role: define the universe and invite contemplation.
- Mood: sacred, spacious, quietly majestic.
- Tempo language: slow pulse or free-time opening moving into a stable loop.
- Melody: recognizable, simple, memorable motif fragments.

### map
- Role: movement across worlds and route choice.
- Mood: exploratory, elevated, lightly prophetic.
- Variants: Hanan brighter air, Kay grounded pulse, Ukhu darker resonance.
- Rhythm: gentle step-like motion, never combat urgency.

### combat
- Role: tactical pressure and forward motion.
- Mood: alert, rhythmic, ceremonial violence.
- Rhythm: hand drums, low pulses, syncopated accents, short motive repetition with variation.
- Melody: bold interval jumps, short phrase calls, no endless one-note ostinato.

### boss
- Role: mythic threat and ritual climax.
- Mood: severe, cosmic, expansive, dangerous.
- Rhythm: slower but heavier and more inevitable than standard combat.
- Harmony: suspended dread, low choir, broken ceremonial brass or horn-synth accents.

### victory
- Role: release, radiance, earned uplift.
- Mood: warm, ascending, noble, brief.
- Melody: small upward contour with clear cadence.

### shop
- Role: pause, curiosity, intimate ritual commerce.
- Mood: lighter, tactile, safe but still sacred.
- Texture: plucked detail, soft bells, low air pad.

### pack_open
- Role: anticipation and reveal.
- Mood: focused, bright, ceremonial tension.
- Shape: quick rise, moment of suspension, clean reveal accent.

### rare_reveal
- Role: reward escalation.
- Mood: bright sacred rarity, not explosive chaos.
- Shape: ascending motif with glittering overtones and controlled low support.

### legendary_reveal
- Role: apex rarity and mythic confirmation.
- Mood: overwhelming but clean, cosmic, solemnly triumphant.
- Shape: broader register span, stronger choir/brass layer, slower final cadence.

### credits
- Role: closure and memory.
- Mood: reflective, wide, human, mythic afterglow.
- Structure: motif restatement in a calmer, more complete form.

## Context-to-Timbre Summary
- studio_intro: airy choir, bell shimmer, soft drone, short flute phrase.
- main_menu: choir pad, flute lead, distant drum heartbeat, wind texture.
- map: breath flute, light percussion, open drones, regional ambience.
- combat: low choir, hybrid brass synth, tribal drums, pulse bass.
- boss: deep choir, ritual horn, heavy drum, void resonance.
- victory: bright choir, bell, upward flute/horn phrase.
- shop: plucked string, hand bell, warm pad, subtle wind.
- pack_open: glass bell, rising swell, short low hit.
- rare_reveal: brighter bell choir, metallic sparkle, compact ceremonial hit.
- legendary_reveal: full sacred choir, wide low hit, radiant shimmer, formal cadence.
- credits: flute, choir, drum pulse softened, expansive air texture.

## Mixing and Loop Rules
- Preserve headroom and avoid fatiguing constant peaks.
- Use dynamic swells to imply ritual breath.
- Low end must support, not blur melodic lines.
- Loop boundary should land after a phrase, not in the middle of a symbolic accent.
- Avoid tail truncation that makes long tracks feel clipped or fake-looped.

## Quality Gate Before Regeneration
- Track must contain melodic motion.
- Track must contain more than one pitch center or phrase color.
- Track must feel context-specific within the first few seconds.
- Ambient layers must enrich context, not replace composition.
- The Chakana motif should be recoverable across menu, combat, boss, and credits.
