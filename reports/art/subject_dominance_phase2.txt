PHASE 2 - SUBJECT DOMINANCE RULES

Summary
- The pipeline now validates subject dominance with real thresholds instead of permissive placeholders.
- Subject occupancy is now measured from full visible subject mass by merging silhouette and detail layers before validation.
- Subject and object layout were both enlarged to push the generator toward stronger character-first scenes.
- Subject staging now adds stronger non-FX figure-ground support behind the character to improve local separation.
- Phase 2 improved the metrics materially, but it did not fully solve stylized character readability for all three archetypes.

Implemented rules
- Required thresholds are now explicit in the pipeline:
  - occ_subject >= 0.22
  - occ_object >= 0.06
  - contrast_score >= 0.62
- Preferred ranges are documented in the pipeline summary:
  - occ_subject 0.28-0.38
  - occ_object 0.08-0.14
- Readability now uses merged subject mass instead of silhouette-only occupancy.
- Figure centering and 40%-60% subject height constraints remain enforced.
- FX limits remain in place and are not relaxed.

Warnings
- The pipeline is now honest about failure: scenes that previously passed because of lax thresholds now fail correctly.
- Phase 2 can increase size and staging, but it cannot fully fix character readability while the subject is still built from simplified geometric anatomy.
- Object occupancy remains especially weak for thin weapons and staff-like props.
- Contrast is still limited because palette and backdrop separation are stronger than body-shape readability itself.
