PHASE 1 - SUBJECT GENERATION AUDIT

Summary
- assembly_pipeline_v1 already uses the requested layer order, but subject dominance is still weak because subject and object are built from sparse procedural geometry and validated with permissive thresholds.
- Lore and tags affect palette, reference sampling and environment choice more than they affect pose, body language, weapon anchoring or scene identity.
- The current system is still closer to symbolic flat composition than to directed stylized fantasy character construction.

Audit Findings

1. How subject geometry is currently built
- Layer order is declared in `game/art/assembly_pipeline.py:29` and executed in `assemble_scene_art()` in `game/art/assembly_pipeline.py`.
- The subject silhouette is created first through `draw_subject_silhouette()` in `game/art/silhouette_builder.py:555`.
- Subject bounds come from `_subject_rect()` in `game/art/silhouette_builder.py:524`.
- The active silhouette helpers are:
  - `generate_humanoid_silhouette()` at `game/art/silhouette_builder.py:459`
  - `generate_archon_silhouette()` at `game/art/silhouette_builder.py:493`
  - `generate_solar_warrior_silhouette()` at `game/art/silhouette_builder.py:511`
- Character detail is then painted by `draw_subject()` in `game/art/silhouette_builder.py:631`.
- Those subject builders still rely mostly on circles, rectangles, polygons and thick lines. The result is a readable symbol, but not yet a directed stylized character body.
- Reference-guided subject and object overlays are disabled because `_blit_reference_subject()` and `_blit_reference_object()` both return `False`.

2. Why subject occupancy is too low
- `occ_subject` is measured from the silhouette layer only inside `validate_readability()` at `game/art/assembly_pipeline.py:319`, not from the combined silhouette + detail mass.
- The silhouette templates leave a lot of empty space inside the subject frame, so the bounding box may be tall enough while alpha-filled area stays low.
- `_subject_rect()` allocates a moderate subject frame, but the actual body often occupies much less than that frame because the shapes are thin or segmented.
- Several variants still read like emblems or totems rather than dominant humanoid bodies, especially the mage and throne-oriented forms.
- Validation no longer requires a real `occ_subject` minimum, even though `assembly_pipeline_summary()` still advertises one.

3. Why object occupancy is too low
- Objects are built in `draw_focus_object()` at `game/art/silhouette_builder.py:696`.
- Object ratios are clamped tightly and then reduced again, so many objects end up visually present but too thin in occupied area.
- Weapons are especially penalized because their long narrow shape produces low alpha occupancy.
- Reference-guided object mass is currently disabled because `_blit_reference_object()` returns `False`.
- The validation rule already compensates for this with a relaxed weapon threshold, which confirms the construction is underfilled.

4. Why contrast is failing
- `_contrast_score()` in `game/art/assembly_pipeline.py:105` uses average luma of the subject/object union against a nearby outer ring. It does not measure edge clarity or local silhouette separation.
- `_strong_foreground_palette()` in `game/art/scene_engine.py:115` strengthens color mood, but it does not guarantee figure-ground separation.
- `_blit_environment_reference()` applies a full-frame environment plate plus tint, glow, shade and veil, which supports mood but can still leave the subject close to the same value family as the background.
- `_draw_subject_staging()` and `_draw_object_staging()` help a little, but they are too subtle to rescue weak body construction.
- `_apply_contrast()` is a global post-pass, not a subject-aware separation pass.

5. How lore and tags are currently interpreted
- Prompt parsing happens in `semantic_from_prompt()` at `game/art/scene_engine.py:30`.
- Tags currently influence:
  - palette and civilization palette
  - reference categories through `_categories_for_prompt()`
  - keyword expansion through `_keywords_from_semantic()` at `game/art/scene_engine.py:161`
  - preferred references through `_prioritize_refs()` at `game/art/scene_engine.py:293`
  - environment preset selection
  - foreground palette bias through `_strong_foreground_palette()`
  - FX family through `draw_fx()` in `game/art/fx_layer.py`
- Tags do not yet strongly control:
  - body shape language
  - stance and pose type
  - dominant arm direction
  - weapon anchor point
  - symbol anchor point
  - lighting direction by archetype
  - aura behavior as part of body readability

6. Missing rules for stylized humanoid readability
- There is no modular body system for archetype-specific parts such as head, torso, cloak/armor mass, dominant arm, support arm, lower body stance, weapon anchor and symbol anchor.
- There is no minimum filled-mass rule for the subject silhouette, only height and centering rules.
- There is no archetype-specific pose library for archon, solar warrior and guide mage.
- There is no explicit body-to-weapon anchoring rule to keep the object readable as part of the pose.
- There is no local contrast rule that guarantees the head and torso separate from the immediate background.
- There is no strong lore-to-shape mapping from tags like `ritual`, `attack`, `support`, `wisdom`, `corruption`, `mountain` or `chakana` into silhouette bias or pose logic.

Warnings
- `assembly_pipeline_summary()` reports `occ_subject_min: 0.20`, but `validate_readability()` does not enforce it.
- The reference system is currently reduced mostly to palette seeding and selection hints because subject and object cutout blending are disabled.
- The pipeline can satisfy centering and height while still failing character readability.
- FX already uses a keepout zone, so the main blocker is under-constructed subject and object form, not overuse of FX.

PASS / WARNING / FAIL
