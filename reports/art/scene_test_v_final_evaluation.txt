scene_test_v_final_evaluation

General verdict
- The pipeline is now stable enough to separate environment, subject, object and FX.
- The metrics are acceptable, but the visual result still shows incomplete figure definition.
- The main failure is no longer pure washout; it is figure construction quality and layer discipline.

What is working
- Composition is solved at low resolution and then upscaled.
- Subject/object/background separation is measurable and mostly stable.
- Weapon attachment is reliable.
- White clipping is controlled.
- The retry loop is already limited to one extra pass.

What is still failing visually
- The character body still reads as a simplified monolithic mass instead of a stylized fantasy figure.
- A tall frontal pale column is still visible in the three tests and weakens torso/head readability.
- Internal shape language is too rectangular in chest and robe/armor zones.
- Symbol and halo placement are improved, but the subject core still lacks enough intentional anatomy cues.
- The pipeline passes metrics earlier than it passes visual readability.

Per-card notes
- ARCHON: the robe mass is readable, but the central pale overlay still flattens the ritual figure.
- SOLAR_WARRIOR: weapon readability is strong, but front FX/overlay pressure pushes the subject close to the visibility threshold.
- GUIDE_MAGE: the silhouette is calmer and cleaner, but internal form remains too block-like.

Priority fixes before larger batches
- Remove the tall frontal neutral overlay from the subject stack or move it to a controlled detail layer.
- Replace torso rectangles with clearer chest, shoulder and pelvis masses.
- Add secondary internal volumes for elbows, forearms and cloak breakups.
- Gate readability on silhouette quality plus visual shape checkpoints, not only occupancy and contrast.
